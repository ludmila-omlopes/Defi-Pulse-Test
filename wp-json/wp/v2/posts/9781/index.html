Today we interview Paul Razvan Berg, previously a part of Aztec Protocol, self-proclaimed “Streamer of Money” and the man behind new Sablier<\/a>.<\/em><\/strong><\/p>\n

How did you get your start in crypto and what inspired you to build Sablier?<\/h3>\n

The bull market of 2017 took me by surprise and I said to myself that there must be some kind of fundamentals behind all the hype. It turned out there was and I’ve been involved with Ethereum and smart contracts ever since.<\/p>\n

I started working on Sablier after getting my paycheck delayed by a few days. Mind you, I was working at a crypto startup, so I asked myself why do we have to use these legacy payment systems when smart contracts can be as fast as … real-time?<\/p>\n

What are you building and what sets it apart?<\/h3>\n

Building Sablier, the protocol for real-time finance. Just like you can stream music on Spotify, so you can stream money on Sablier.<\/p>\n

As far as I know, we are the first Ethereum project to have implemented a money streaming protocol that is production-ready and is accompanied by a nice user interface.<\/p>\n
